About Himachal Pradesh

Himachal Pradesh, known as the "Dev Bhoomi" (Land of Gods), is a beautiful state in northern India. It was formed on 25 January 1971. Surrounded by the mighty Himalayas, it is famous for its breathtaking natural beauty, charming hill stations, and vibrant cultural heritage that dates back centuries.

Culture & Festivals

Himachal Pradesh is rich in culture with traditional dance, music, and colorful festivals. Popular festivals include Kullu Dussehra, Losar, and Renuka Fair. People wear vibrant Himachali caps and traditional dresses. The Nati dance, performed during celebrations, is recognized by UNESCO as one of the largest folk dances in the world.

Famous Food

Siddu 🥟

Siddu is a type of steamed bun prepared by fermenting the dough. Usually made with flour, it can be eaten in both sweet and savory forms. In Himachal, it is made in three to four different ways and is a beloved mountain delicacy.

Himachali Dham 🍛

Himachali Dham is a traditional culinary event that delights taste buds and symbolizes the region's rich cultural heritage. It is a traditional mid-day meal served during weddings, birthdays, and religious ceremonies.

Madra 🥘

Madra is a Himachali curry made with yogurt and legumes. The recipe usually includes white chickpeas, kidney beans, and black-eyed peas. It is one of the dishes served during the traditional Himachali food festival called Dham.

Babru 🍘

Babru is a popular breakfast delicacy similar to traditional poori but with a unique twist - it has stuffing inside! Filled with urad dal (black gram) filling, it adds a delightful flavor that makes it a must-try Himachali snack.
